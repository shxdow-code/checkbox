Aeronautical Knowledge

This encompasses the fundamental principles of aviation that UAS operators need to understand.

It includes

  • knowledge of weather
  • airspace classification
  • navigation
  • the principles of flight

Operators must also be familiar with the regulations governing UAS operations.

Systems Knowledge

This involves understanding the components and systems that comprise a UAS, including

  • the airframe
  • propulsion
  • control systems
  • communication systems
  • payloads

It also includes knowledge of how these systems interact and function together.

Mission Planning

The process of planning a UAS operation, including

  • defining the mission objectives
  • assessing the operational environment
  • selecting appropriate equipment
  • planning the flight path
  • establishing contingency plans

It ensures that the mission can be carried out safely and efficiently.

Maintenance

The procedures and practices involved in keeping a UAS safe and operational.

This includes

  • routine inspections
  • troubleshooting
  • repairs
  • keeping maintenance records.

Proper maintenance ensures the longevity and reliability of the UAS

VLOS Flight Training

Training that focuses on operating a UAS within the visual line of sight of the operator.

This includes

  • understanding how to maintain visual contact with the aircraft
  • run checklists, manage flight maneuvers, maximize the payload
  • handle any immediate issues that arise during the flight.

Human Factors

The study of how humans interact with the systems they control.

This includes

  • understanding operators' physical and cognitive limitations
  • usability of controls and interfaces
  • training operators to manage stress and workload effectively.

Safety Management

Identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks associated with UAS operations.

It involves

  • creating and implementing safety policies and procedures
  • conducting safety audits
  • promoting a safety culture within the organization.

Internal Control Station Operation

The procedures and skills required to operate the control station from which the UAS is piloted.

This includes

  • understanding the layout and functions of the control station software
  • interpreting the control interfaces
  • managing internal external communications.

BVLOS Flight Training

Training that prepares operators to control a UAS when it is beyond their direct visual line of sight.

This includes:

  • Using advanced navigation and detection and avoidance technologies
  • managing long-distance communication links
  • Understanding the regulatory requirements for BVLOS operations
